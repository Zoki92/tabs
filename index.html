Independent
Independent Accommodation Independent accommodation within the private rented sector Evolve acts as a social lettings agency providing good quality and affordable private accommodation with integrated support. Evolve manages a wide range of private rented accommodation across Norfolk. This enables us to ensure we can provide safe and comfortable accommodation of a high standard where the young person wants to live. This accommodation may be sole occupancy, shared accommodation with floating support, or shared accommodation with integrated support via a peer landlord. It is our aim that, over time, our young people will achieve the skills required to manage their tenancies successfully, thus allowing them to remain our tenants even when we are no longer actively supporting them in other areas. The size of our housing stock means that we are able to make a long term commitment to our tenants and will be able to provide accommodation opportunities as their lives move on and accommodation needs change.
Supported Lodgings
Supported Lodgings Evolve recruits a range of people who are able to offer accommodation within their own homes and a level of support appropriate to the needs of the young person. Our supported lodgings providers are assessed, vetted, and trained to a high standard. As an organisation we use the fostering regulations and national minimum standards to guide our training and assessment process. Our providers have access to 24 hour support and advice 7 days a week, 52 weeks of the year. This level of support enables our supported lodgings providers to offer, when required, a high level of support to vulnerable young people with additional needs. Our out of hour’s service also means that we are able to consider emergency placements. However, it is our expectation that most of the young people we work with will come to us in a planned and prepared manner.
Taster Tenancies
Taster Tenancies For young people who are unsure whether they are ready for independent accommodation, we can offer a tenancy trial. In most cases this will be for a period of at least two weeks. The young person’s usual accommodation must remain available to them during this period.
Flexible Accommodation
Flexibility in Accommodation Provision We appreciate that sometimes young people’s circumstances and accommodation needs change. We will be able to respond to this whether their needs increase or decrease, or if they require a move to a different type of accommodation or area. It is our expectation that some of our young people may move through our accommodation services as part of a planned process, perhaps starting in supported lodgings, moving to shared accommodation and then to their own tenancy when they feel ready and able to manage this level of responsibility. It is our desire to be a flexible landlord, and as such we will seek to facilitate a rapid response in situations where a young person is not coping. Therefore such tenancies may be terminated by mutual consent during the fixed term, where it is in the best interests of the young person and is not detrimental to the viability of our business.
